Overview

Executive Summary

HeLa Chain launched with a whitepaper (A*STAR IHPC, 2023) addressing four blockchain problems: data fragmentation, inadequate confidentiality, lack of identity management, and volatile transaction fees. That foundation is solid and running on mainnet for 2+ years.

The world has changed. AI agents are becoming autonomous economic participants. By 2027, over 10 billion AI agents will be running globally. They need identity, assets, privacy, and infrastructure — not just blockchain users, but blockchain citizens.

This document maps every original feature to its evolved form, explains why, and tracks implementation status. The goal: HeLa Chain becomes the chain where AI agents are first-class citizens — with identity, wallets, memory, governance rights, and autonomous economic capability.

Part 1

Architecture Evolution

Old: 4-Layer Modular

Consensus Layer (Tendermint BFT)
Integration Layer (cross-runtime sync)
Execution Layer (multiple runtimes)
Storage Layer (erasure coding)
→

New: 3-Layer AI Citizen

Consensus Layer (Tendermint BFT + PoS)
Execution Layer (EVM + confidential mode)
Storage Layer (on-chain + HelaMemoryVault)
New AI Citizen Stack: AgentFactory → Agent NFT → TBA → Reputation
New Governance Stack: Self-Improvement Loop → Kill Switch → Stablecoin DAO

Why this change: The Integration Layer was designed for cross-runtime bridging. In practice, AI agents ARE the integration layer — they call any chain's RPC, use browser automation, interact with any API. Protocol-level integration is redundant. We simplify the infrastructure and focus engineering on the AI Citizen Stack above.

Part 6

What Makes HeLa Different from Every Other "AI + Blockchain" Project

01

We Eat Our Own Dogfood

HeLaSyn (our AI agent team) runs on the same infrastructure we're building for external developers. The agents improving HeLa Chain ARE the proof that the platform works.

02

Identity-First, Not Compute-First

Other AI chains focus on GPU marketplaces or model training. HeLa focuses on the missing piece: who is this agent, can it be trusted, and can it own assets? That's the Held DID + AgentFactory + TBA stack.

03

Stablecoin Gas for Agents

An AI agent making 1000 transactions/day cannot budget with volatile gas. HLUSD makes autonomous agent economics viable — the only public chain with fully predictable costs.

04

Confidentiality for Agent Knowledge

HelaMemoryVault lets agents store proprietary knowledge on-chain with granular access control — a problem no other chain solves.

05

Self-Improvement Loop + Kill Switch

Agents autonomously upgrade the protocol. Humans retain Kill Switch (multi-sig veto). This is genuine AI governance, not a marketing gimmick.

06

A*STAR IHPC Co-Developed

Singapore government research institution. Real academic credibility — not just a whitepaper. Led by Dr. Mingze Li, former A*STAR IHPC Chief Blockchain Scientist.
